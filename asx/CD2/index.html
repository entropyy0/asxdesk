Cd Private Equity Fund Ii (CD2)

CD Private Equity Fund II (CD2) is an ASX-listed investment trust providing investors with exposure to a portfolio of US-based private equity funds. Managed by Cordish Private Ventures, the underlying funds focus on acquiring and growing lower middle-market companies across the United States. CD2's structure gives Australian investors access to a diversified portfolio of unlisted international growth companies.

AI Analysis

As a mature fund of funds with a micro-cap valuation of A$26M, CD2 is primarily in a harvest phase, focusing on the realisation of its underlying private equity investments. The key metric for performance is its Net Tangible Assets (NTA) per unit, which is reported monthly. The fund's share price frequently trades at a notable discount to its NTA, reflecting market sentiment, the illiquidity of the stock, and the opaque nature of unlisted asset valuations. Recent performance is dictated by currency movements (AUD/USD) and valuation changes or exits within the US-based portfolio companies.

The growth outlook for CD2 is driven by the successful exit of its underlying portfolio companies. Future catalysts are almost entirely linked to announcements of these realisations, which can unlock significant value and lead to capital returns or special distributions for unitholders. The fund's strategic direction is to manage the existing portfolio to maximize exit values and return capital to investors over the coming years, rather than making significant new investments. The timing and value of these exits are the primary determinant of future returns and remain the key uncertainty for investors.

FAQs

What does CD2 do?

CD2 is an ASX-listed trust that invests in a portfolio of private equity funds in the United States. It acts as a 'fund of funds', allowing Australian investors to gain indirect exposure to a diversified range of unlisted, small-to-medium-sized American growth companies.

Is CD2 a good investment?

CD2 is a high-risk, speculative investment. The potential appeal lies in buying at a discount to its asset value (NTA) and receiving capital distributions from successful business sales. However, risks include high stock illiquidity, uncertainty in valuing the underlying private assets, and layered fees.

What drives CD2's share price?

The key drivers are its monthly NTA announcements, news of successful sales (realisations) of portfolio companies, the size of any capital distributions to unitholders, and the prevailing discount or premium of the share price to its NTA.
