FAQs

What does ASX do?

ASX Limited operates Australia's main securities and derivatives exchange. It provides the platform for companies to list and for investors to trade stocks, bonds, and other financial products, and also runs the critical clearing and settlement system (CHESS) that finalises these transactions.

Is ASX a good investment?

ASX can be considered a stable, blue-chip investment due to its monopoly-like position in market infrastructure and reliable dividend payments. However, its growth is tied to market cycles, and it faces significant execution risk and potential reputational damage from the ongoing challenges with its CHESS replacement project.

What drives ASX's share price?

The key drivers for ASX's share price include overall market trading volumes (higher volumes mean more fee revenue), the health of the IPO market, interest rate levels (which affect returns on collateral held), and market sentiment regarding the progress and cost of the critical CHESS replacement project.
