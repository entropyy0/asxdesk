Touch Ventures Limited (TVL)

Touch Ventures Limited is an ASX-listed investment holding company focused on high-growth, retail innovation and consumer-focused businesses. Spun out of Afterpay, its primary asset is a strategic holding in the US business of Afterpay, now part of Block, Inc. (ASX: SQ2). The company also holds investments in other unlisted technology companies such as parcel delivery service Sendle and open banking platform Basiq.

AI Analysis

Touch Ventures' current position is overwhelmingly dictated by its investment in Block, Inc., which constitutes the vast majority of its Net Tangible Assets (NTA). Consequently, the company's recent performance has mirrored the significant decline in Block's share price from its post-pandemic highs, leading to a sharp fall in TVL's own valuation. A key metric for investors is the company's share price relative to its reported NTA per share; TVL has consistently traded at a substantial discount to its NTA, reflecting market concerns about its concentrated portfolio, management fees, and lack of direct control over its primary asset.

The growth outlook for TVL is almost entirely dependent on a recovery in the Block, Inc. share price and a broader resurgence in the fintech sector. The primary catalyst for shareholder returns would be a significant appreciation of its Block holding. Strategically, the company's direction involves managing its existing portfolio and potentially providing follow-on funding. Future catalysts could include a liquidity event (like an IPO or trade sale) for one of its unlisted investments like Sendle, which would validate its investment thesis beyond Block and provide a non-correlated source of value.

Bull Case

  • • A recovery in the Block, Inc. (SQ2) share price would directly and significantly increase TVL's NTA, providing a powerful tailwind for its own share price.
  • • The large and persistent discount to NTA could narrow if the market regains confidence in fintech or if the company undertakes capital management initiatives like a share buy-back.
  • • A successful exit or significant valuation uplift from its other portfolio companies (e.g., Sendle, Basiq) could unlock value and diversify its return profile away from Block.

Bear Case

  • • Extreme portfolio concentration risk, with the company's valuation being almost entirely dependent on the performance of a single listed stock, Block, Inc.
  • • The significant discount to NTA may persist indefinitely as the market may continue to penalise the company for its management costs and lack of liquidity.
  • • The value of its unlisted assets is illiquid and subject to writedowns, with no guarantee of a successful exit to realise their carrying value.

FAQs

What does TVL do?

Touch Ventures (TVL) is an investment company that holds a portfolio of fintech and retail technology businesses. Its most significant asset is a holding in the US business of Afterpay, which is now part of the listed global technology company Block, Inc.

Is TVL a good investment?

TVL is a speculative investment that offers indirect exposure to Block, Inc., often at a discount to the underlying asset value. The potential for this discount to close presents an opportunity, but investors face significant risks, including high concentration in a single volatile stock (Block), management fees, and the illiquid nature of its smaller private investments.

What drives TVL's share price?

The primary driver of TVL's share price is the market performance of Block, Inc. (ASX: SQ2), as this directly impacts its Net Tangible Assets (NTA). Other key factors include changes in the discount to NTA, news from its unlisted portfolio companies, and overall investor sentiment towards the technology and 'buy now, pay later' sectors.
